Most growing companies start with a spreadsheet or a personal expense app. That works until you have more than a handful of people spending company money — and a CA asking for GST-ready exports every month.

## Tracking vs controlling

**Tracking** answers: “What did we spend?”  
**Spend management** answers: “Should this spend have happened — and can finance trust it?”

Expense tracking is a rear-view mirror. Spend management puts **policy, approvals, and tax-ready data** in front of the spend so problems don’t pile up at month-end.

## Why Indian SMBs feel this pain first

- Multiple people booking travel, tools, and vendor payments
- Receipts scattered across WhatsApp and email
- GST splits guessed after the fact
- No clear “who approved this?” trail when something looks off

A personal tracker wasn’t built for that. A company spend system was.

## What “good” looks like

1. **Policy at the point of spend** — limits and receipt rules checked when someone submits
2. **Approvals finance trusts** — a clear queue and an audit trail
3. **GST-ready from day one** — tax fields captured with the expense, not reconstructed later
4. **One workspace** — employees, managers, and finance stop juggling five tools

## Tracking is still useful — it’s just not enough

You still need reports and history. Spend management doesn’t throw that away; it adds control **before** numbers hit your books.

## Next step

If your team is past “one person and a spreadsheet,” put policy and approvals in front of spend — then let reports show you what’s under control.
