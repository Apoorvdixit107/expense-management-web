# A simple expense policy your startup can enforce this week

Long policy PDFs don’t control spend. **Short rules that the product enforces** do.

Here’s a starter policy you can adopt in a few days — then tighten as you grow.

## 1. Categories that need a receipt

Require a receipt/invoice for:

- Any spend above a threshold (e.g. ₹500 or ₹1,000 — pick one)  
- All travel, lodging, and client entertainment  
- Any vendor payment you may claim input tax credit on  

No receipt → not reimbursable / not approvable.

## 2. Limits people can understand

Examples (adjust to your stage):

| Category | Soft guidance | Hard limit (needs extra approval) |
|----------|---------------|-------------------------------------|
| Team meals | Reasonable | ₹X per person |
| Local travel | Metro/cab as needed | ₹Y per day |
| Software tools | Pre-approved list | Anything new → finance first |

Publish the table in Slack **and** encode the hard limits in your spend tool.

## 3. One clear approval path

For early teams, one level is enough:

- Employee submits  
- Manager or finance approves  
- Only then does it count as trusted spend  

Avoid “anyone can mark it paid in the sheet.” That’s how shadow spending starts.

## 4. What happens when someone breaks the rule

Say it plainly:

- Missing receipt → returned for fix  
- Over limit without approval → rejected or escalated  
- Repeat issues → manager conversation, not silent spreadsheet edits  

## 5. Roll it out in 48 hours

1. Write the one-page policy (this article is your outline)  
2. Announce in Slack with the table of limits  
3. Turn on receipt + limit rules in ExpenseKit  
4. Run the approval queue for one week and adjust  

## Bottom line

A policy nobody enforces is decoration. A short policy your system checks on every submission is spend management.
