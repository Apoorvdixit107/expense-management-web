# GST on company expenses in India: a practical checklist for SMBs

> **Not tax advice.** This is an operational checklist for finance teams and founders. Confirm treatment with your CA for your business.

Messy GST data is one of the fastest ways month-end turns into a fire drill. The fix is usually not a clever formula — it’s capturing the right fields **when the expense is submitted**.

## Capture this on every company expense

- [ ] Vendor / merchant name  
- [ ] Invoice or receipt date  
- [ ] Taxable value and GST amount (or a clear “GST inclusive” total)  
- [ ] GST rate when known (e.g. 5% / 12% / 18%)  
- [ ] Whether GST is **inclusive** or **exclusive** of the amount entered  
- [ ] Place of supply / state when your process needs it  
- [ ] Attachment: clear photo or PDF of the invoice  

If any of these are missing, your future self (or your CA) will pay for it in cleanup time.

## Inclusive vs exclusive — say it out loud

Teams get burned when one person enters ₹1,180 as “the amount” and another assumes that was pre-GST.

**Rule of thumb for your policy:**

- Require submitters to mark **inclusive** or **exclusive**  
- Prefer systems that split tax automatically from that choice  
- Don’t leave “figure it out in Excel later” as the default  

## What finance should review weekly

- Expenses missing receipts  
- Odd GST rates for the category  
- Vendors without invoices  
- Large spends that skipped approval  

A short weekly review beats a painful quarterly reconstruction.
